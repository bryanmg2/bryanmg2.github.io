Conclusion

Based on the data, we can conclude that these 5 institutions of higher education in Oregon are legitimate schools that confer bachelor's degrees and invest more money in their students' education than they receive in revenue from the students:

  • Oregon Health & Science University
  • Oregon Institute of Technology
  • Portland State University
  • Southern Oregon University
  • Western Oregon University

To answer Steve Jobs' question, "is it worth it" for working-class parents to send their kids to these schools? Unequivocally, yes: The State of Oregon, despite its meager resources, is essentially paying people to get degrees at these institutions as an investment in the State's future success. In the case of OHSU, Oregon invests almost $50,000/yr. per Student for Oregonians to go to medical school, study Bioinformatics, or get a degree in a field related to Healthcare. That's an amazing benefit no matter what State you live in.

Having said that, it would be incorrect to assume the other schools in Oregon don't provide value. Reed is an amazing college, even if Steve Jobs couldn't see the value in the money his parents were spending at the time. He went on to say of his experience at Reed, "much of what I stumbled into by following my curiosity and intuition turned out to be priceless later on."1 My goal in demonstrating the value of these 5 colleges is to convince prospective students of their value up front, so when they come to those difficult moments in their college years, they already know how to value their experiences, even before they come to realize the priceless value of their education later in life.
