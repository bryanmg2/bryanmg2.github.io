2-Year Colleges vs. 4-Year Colleges

Of the 22 institutions of higher education in Oregon that provide subsidized education, only 5 are 4-Year Colleges:

When picking a college, it is important to know what your academic goals are. If you goal is to get a Bachelor's degree -- a pre-requisite for most graduate school programs and an internationally recognized credential (Associate degrees are not1) -- then, you should have in mind a 4-year school, even if you intend to go to a 2-Year college first. The reason is that some 2-Year schools have special arrangements to transfer to a 4-year school once you've completed a defined series of courses. Even if they don't, you still want to be sure the classes you take at a 2-year college will fully transfer to the 4-year college.

Therefore, realistically the choice comes down to 5 schools, each of which is a 4-year college that provides subsidized education beyond the money the students and their famillies invest themselves.
