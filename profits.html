Subsidized financial benefit to the Student

This chart shows the expenditures1 per student minus the revenues1, so students can see if they will actually be getting more financial benefit from their education than they are spending on it.

In some cases, the schools are spending much more on their students than they are getting from them. For example, OHSU is spending almost $50,000/yr. more on their students than the students are bringing to the school in the form of revenue.

Each of the schools that provide subsidized education beyond what the students spent is worth looking into for the value they will bring to their students. Because Oregon is a very large State, it would also help to know where the schools are located.

1 College Scorecard Data. U.S. Department of Education. Retrieved July 5, 2020 from https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/data/
